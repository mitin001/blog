## To be human is to struggle, and that's wonderful

> Heartbreak and struggle is what fuels honesty and emotion and artistry.

[Lady GaGa: I'm an Artist, Not a Celebrity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OJEqHFEY3FI), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=a09590d5476b37ede06c7efb86ee3dbc).

It's not that rejection and sacrifice are unpleasant but unavoidable side effects of exposing yourself to enough of life's situations as to eventually stumble into a lucky break. No, rejection and sacrifice are the point. They mold you into a person who, presented with an opportunity, can seize on it and not waste it. Rejection and sacrifice give you the discipline and perseverance you need to see it through. They make you into an interesting person. They give your output that inimitable hook that makes others pay attention. It doesn't matter if your undertaking is an art or a science. These are both sides of the same human equation. And to be human is to grow through and despite adversity.
