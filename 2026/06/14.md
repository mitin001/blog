## Analytic vs. synthetic approach to philosophy

> The human brain is full of pipes. ... You know how philosophers have these thoughts, and they try and push these thoughts through these pipes? When you're having a big idea, is it best to break it up into lots of little thoughts, about the size of peas, and squeeze them through
in quick succession, or just bite the bullet and force it through your mind pipe in one huge clod, like gritting your teeth and thinking for dear life?

[Cunk On Earth, Episode 1 - In The Beginnings], [transcript](https://www.springfieldspringfield.co.uk/view_episode_scripts.php?tv-show=cunk-on-earth-2022&episode=s01e01).

Don't try to explain to me the big picture. Give me a set of simple ideas or examples, and the big picture will start to emerge in my head. You can't shove the big picture into my head any other way. It won't fit. The analysis happens outside my head, in me using tools like pen and paper or a computer or in asking questions to reduce ideas into what will fit, and the synthesis happens inside my head, as these ideas aggregate into one big-picture whole.
