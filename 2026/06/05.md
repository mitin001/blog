## Lesson from Bayrob: when you're not focused, your brain may be spilling privileged information to wiretaps

> One of the members of the group was typing in his e-mail address to log in on gmx.de or one-on-one internet. They did not use SSL at the time for the log-in form. So, when he typed in his e-mail address he typed in his personal e-mail address and then went, _oops_, and then logged in with his “work” e-mail address.

[Darknet Diaries. Ep 175: Bayrob].

Eoin Miller, an AOL employee, was able to determine the personal email address of one of Bayrob members when he saw him mistakenly submit the wrong email address in the login form before almost immediately submitting the correct one, all while using the AOL proxy. Sometimes one's brain briefly switches into the autopilot mode, and if actions taken during such time are eavesdropped and logged, they could be studied to reveal privileged information the person wouldn't otherwise want to divulge. Our Freudian slips are windows into our most private desires. Non-Freudean slips can be even worse. One could offer to drive a friend to the airport but, in a laid-back haze of conversation, would end up driving them to work instead, and if they happen to be working at an off-grid military base, that would make it all the more awkward. Sometimes all it takes is one slip-up.
