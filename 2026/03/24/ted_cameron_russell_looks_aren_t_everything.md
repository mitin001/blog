# [TED. Cameron Russell: Looks aren't everything. Believe me, I'm a model](https://www.ted.com/talks/cameron_russell_looks_aren_t_everything_believe_me_i_m_a_model)

This TED talk was recommended to me by [YouTube](../../../2025/10/17/youtube.md). It answered a question about the modeling profession.

## How does one become a model?

Based on how well you happen to fit society's beauty standards and whether you have any desire to exploit that for the sake of your economic security, says a working model.

> I won a genetic lottery, and I am the recipient of a legacy.

Society's beauty standards are different from what makes people attractive from the biological point of view (e.g., how healthy you look).

> For the past few centuries we have defined beauty not just as health and youth and symmetry that we're biologically programmed to admire, but also as tall, slender figures, and femininity and white skin. And this is a legacy that was built for me, and it's a legacy that I've been cashing out on.

Society's beauty standards aren't good for a diverse and healthy society, and even those whom society _has_ admitted into the modeling profession acknowledge that.

> I felt very uncomfortable to come out here and say, "Look l've received all these benefits from a deck stacked in my favor," and it also felt really uncomfortable to follow that up with, "and it doesn't always make me happy." But mostly it was difficult to unpack a legacy of gender and racial oppression when I am one of the biggest beneficiaries.
