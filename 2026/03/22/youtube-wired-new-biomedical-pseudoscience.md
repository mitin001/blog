## What's the difference between prebiotics and probiotics?

Probiotics are supplements that contain commensal bacterial cultures that, when ingested, will join the ecosystem of your gut, potentially improving it. Prebiotics are the food for said bacteria, that is, fiber.

> Prebiotic is really just a fancy word for fiber. So prebiotics are the food that the bacteria in your gut are gonna use to do what they do. While you might see supplements that say they're filled with prebiotics, it's really just saying they're containing different types of fiber. And so you can get a lot of those health benefits that we know we need from fiber just by eating some fresh fruits and vegetables.

Nutritional supplement industry is trying to use big words to baffle people into buying from them (at a premium price) what they are already getting from regular food.

## Where did the misconception that sugar promotes cancer growth come from?

A tumor, like any other living tissue, uses sugar as fuel. A tumor grows faster than most other tissues, so it consumes sugar faster. This fact has been exploited in imaging studies of cancer. Conspiracy theorists looked at those studies without giving them a due critical evaluation and came away from them with a new conspiracy theory: Big Food is putting sugar into food to make us so sick that we start relying on Big Pharma.

> On a PET scan we use a radioactive labeled sugar that's taken up by your body, and it lights up in regions where cells in your body are metabolizing that sugar more quickly. Because cancer cells do have faster metabolic activity, because they're growing very quickly, unfortunately, this has been misinterpreted to lead people to believe that sugar itself is causing cancer when it's just a feature of certain cancer types. Cancer cells will consume glucose at a faster rate because they need to take that glucose and make new cell components because those tumors are growing very quickly. So it's not the sugar that's feeding or causing cancer, it's a function of how cancer cells are using sugar that's available.

## Where did the misconception that vaccines contain toxic substances come from? 

Conspiracy theorists learned that a preservative used in some vaccines, thimerosal, was a salt of ethylmercury, which contains a mercury ion. Since some compounds that contain mercury ions are toxic, said conspiracy theorists jumped to the false conclusion that all compounds that contain mercury ions are toxic, thereby decrying all vaccines that contain thimerosal as toxic.

> None of the vaccines contain mercury. There's a preservative that's used to prevent contamination of vaccines with things like bacteria and fungi that is called thimerosal. And thimerosal is a salt of a different compound called ethylmercury. Now, ethylmercury is not mercury. It's a compound that contains a mercury ion. This was conflated to suggest that vaccines contained mercury. It's also further confusing because there's a different type of a mercury compound called methylmercury. That's the one that we're warned about eating too much fish like tuna, because the methylmercury is found everywhere, and that can accumulate in our bodies.

Just as not every alcohol is the same in terms of toxicity (ethanol can be ingested in moderation whereas rubbing alcohol is toxic), not every substance that contains mercury ions is the same in terms of toxicity. Unlike methylmercury, ethylmercury does not accumulate in the body; it is quickly cleared from the body.

> Ethanol is safe at relatively low doses, and many people enjoy a drink or two, but nobody's gonna drink a bottle of rubbing alcohol because we know that that would be toxic. The same is true for methylmercury versus ethylmercury. One of them can be harmful at a relatively low dose, and the other one can be safe at a relatively low dose. So thimerosal was used in several vaccines to prevent contamination, but thimerosal is not mercury. However, because of misunderstanding about this chemical difference, most vaccines ended up removing the thimerosal outright.

But that just gave conspiracy theorists more fodder for their conspiracy theories: since Big Pharma is removing thimerosal from vaccines, that must mean that the substance was indeed found to be toxic, when, in reality, vaccine manufacturers simply decided that the ingredient could be substituted with one that wasn't something concerned parents kept avoiding due to the antivax propaganda they stumbled upon while scrolling through social media.
