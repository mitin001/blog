# [HyperPhysics](http://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/hbase/hph.html)

Often times when I search the Internet for a question related to physics, I end up on HyperPhysics. It explains physics the best way: by relating concepts to each other and letting the reader discover how they fit together, often by thinking through the units involved in the physical quantities measured in physical experiments (e.g., to jump 10 cm in the air, a person weighing 100 kg must overcome 100 kg * 10 m/s² = 1 kN gravity over 0.1 m, i.e., they need to expend at least 100 J of energy).
