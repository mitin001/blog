## Why is power measured per second cubed?

For the same reason velocity is measured per second: whereas velocity is position change per second, acceleration is velocity change per second (position change per second per second, i.e., position change per second squared), and power is work change per second. Because work is just acceleration times mass times distance moved, power is related to position change per second (velocity) per second (acceleration) per second, i.e., it is related to position change per second cubed. (Multiply position change per second cubed by mass of the object and distance moved to get the power.)

The rate of doing work (power) can also be viewed as the rate of using energy.
