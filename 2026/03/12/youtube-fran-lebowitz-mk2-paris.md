# [Fran Lebowitz at MK2 Bibliothèque, Paris, 11th March 2022](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MPIt0oLCaGc)

This video was recommended to me by [YouTube](../../../2025/10/17/youtube.md). Its [transcript](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=333c4c4780988720cc7ce6d884d346c8) answered two questions about talent.

## If it is easy for you to prolifically churn out books, does that mean they're bad books?

Not necessarily. Fran Lebowitz seems to think that the rate of writing has nothing to do with its quality. Only your talent determines the quality of your (large or small) output.

> It is just as hard to write a bad book as a good book. The only difference is talent.

## Is obsession with fame healthy for society?

No, this is how society loses order. Andy Warhol could never have predicted this, says Fran Lebowitz.

> What is someone writing their name on something? Either it's vandalism or it's an autograph. And it's a fine line. OK, so kids go on the street and they spray graffiti and it's a crime. You know, Andy signs something and makes it more valuable. I would prefer the graffiti. You talk about the fame. It seems that today fame is more important than talent. More important than anything.

Or, at least, this is how society loses talent. If we prioritize fame-seeking behaviors over excellence, we crowd out the excellence. Public discourse becomes a shouting match on social media. Our information diet is no longer curated by merit but by the algorithm. Our leaders are elected not by their qualifications but by how loud they are.
