# [James O'Brien meets Miriam Margolyes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dh-GJfUqJ6s)

This video was recommended to me by [YouTube](../../../2025/10/17/youtube.md). Its [transcript](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=40afc71823e1ceb9f9484e70b60a1316) answered two questions about getting away from the superficial.

## Why are celebrities interested in hearing non-celebrities talk about their lives?

Because celebrities know that they are famous for superficial reasons. They seek depth in those who don't seek fame. At least, Miriam Margolyes does.

> What I have to offer is not that considerable.

## Does the breadth of one's vocabulary correlate to the depth on one's mind? 

Yes, but this generalization does not apply to the people who learn big words deliberately and superficially (to impress others). What correlates with one's knowledge is the accidental discovery of new words on their path toward acquiring said knowledge, for if the number of such accidents is large, so is the knowledge amassed.

> Words are the currency of thought.

When one seeks to expand their mind, learning new words is inevitable.
