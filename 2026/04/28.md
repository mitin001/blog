## Scheduling threads for cache affinity

> When thread A has run for a long time on CPU k, CPU k’s cache will be full of A’s blocks. If A gets to run again soon, it may perform better if it is run on CPU k, because k’s cache may still contain some of A’s blocks. Having cache blocks preloaded will increase the cache hit rate and thus the thread’s speed. In addition, the TLB may also contain the right pages, reducing TLB faults. Some multiprocessors take this effect into account and use what is called affinity scheduling. ... When a thread is created, it is assigned to a CPU, for example, based on which one has the smallest load at that moment. This assignment of threads to CPUs is the top level of the algorithm. As a result, each CPU acquires its own collection of threads. ... By trying to keep a thread on the same CPU for its entire lifetime, cache affinity is maximized.

[Modern Operating Systems], sec.8.1.4.

When a previously-run thread needs to continue running, the operating system will try to assign it to the core that ran it previously because that core's cache may still contain that thread's data.

## How to visualize a many-dimensional hypercube

<img width="400" alt="1777399046838296388644764120286" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/39863ca8-9b9d-47a3-9ef2-415f4caac66e" />

> We have a four-dimensional cube built from two three- dimensional cubes with the corresponding nodes connected. We could make a five-dimensional cube by cloning the structure ... and connecting the corresponding nodes to form a block of four cubes. To go to six dimensions, we could replicate the block of four cubes and interconnect the corresponding nodes, and so on. An n-dimensional cube formed this way is called a hypercube.

[Modern Operating Systems], sec.8.2.1.

An n-dimensional structure can be created by replicating an (n-1)-dimensional structure. A 2D square replicates a 1D line: connect the two ends of one line to the corresponding two ends of the other line. A 3D cube replicates a 2D square in the same way: connect the two squares. A 4D hypercube replicates a 3D cube in the same way: connect every point of one cube to its corresponding point in the other cube. A 5D hypercube replicates a 4D hypercube in the same way. And so on.

## Network topologies: grid vs. hypercube

<img width="300" alt="17773995049625518880032207135076" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f1d5a41b-d256-4bd3-9ce3-e89052c5df19" />

<img width="300" alt="1777399459164819353778290238857" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6beb7e37-84f3-4728-9628-58257e22e884" />


> Many parallel computers use a hypercube topology because the diameter grows linearly with the dimensionality. Put in other words, the diameter is the base 2 logarithm of the number of nodes. For example, a 10-dimensional hypercube has 1024 nodes but a diameter of only 10, giving excellent delay properties. Note that in contrast, 1024 nodes arranged as a 32 × 32 grid have a diameter of 62, more than six times worse than the hypercube. The price paid for the smaller diameter is that the fanout, and thus the number of links (and the cost), is much larger for the hypercube.

[Modern Operating Systems], sec.8.2.1.

Use the grid topology when the priority is to reduce the number of links. Use the hypercube topology when the priority is to reduce the number of hops.

## The difference between film and television music

> While it may be able to use pop songs in a cognate manner with films, it was never dominated by the convention of music being scored to action as has been the case with feature films. ... The nature of television itself has set severe limitations upon music and its reproduction. The medium has been dominated by sets with very small speakers that are inadequate for good musical sound. Small speakers have less of a frequency spectrum, creating a middle-based sound that is unable to be subtle and lacks the wide dynamics characteristic of feature film sound production.

[Pop music as stock soundtrack to the small screen].

Film production is an engine for music composition. Its action sequences often serve as blueprints for beats, pictures, rhythms, and chords in its score. Film composers are motivated to go all out on film music because they know that their work will go on to play on some of the best and largest speakers available—in movie theaters all over. Film music sometimes doesn't play well outside the theater because it wasn't intended to play on anything smaller. Meanwhile, some makers of television may be so invested in their product they give it the same regard they would reserve for film-making. In their ambition, they may be compelled to borrow film soundtracks to edit into what they're making for the small screen, however they may soon discover that film music isn't sufficient to score television. It often must be supplemented with library, pop, and/or original music made specifically for the smaller screen.
