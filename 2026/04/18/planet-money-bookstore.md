# [Bookstore Economics](https://www.npr.org/transcripts/nx-s1-5751214)

This episode of [Planet Money](../../../2025/10/23/planet-money.md) answered a question about econ terminology.

## When someone says that you're playing "a game of thin margins", what do they mean? 

They mean your business operates like an independent bookstore.

> Order too few copies of a popular new book, and you'll be depriving your store of much-needed financial lifeblood. Order too many, and you'll be clogging up coveted shelf space that could be holding more lucrative titles.

The margin is so thin it can't absorb mispredicted demand. A discount retailer will move inventory onto the clearance rack to try to make room for new inventory that will make it a bulk of its revenue. A restaurant must size its tables, ingredients, and workforce just right to accommodate peak demand during the lunch or dinner rush. A web service similarly must size the resources it's leasing from the cloud just right to ensure the service remains responsive and performant during peak hours.
