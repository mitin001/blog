## How production music ends up in a film

> Temp tracks for films that can sometimes in the final analysis be preferred to the music composed for the film by a commissioned composer.

[Pop music as stock soundtrack to the small screen].

Perhaps, this is how the track [Praying Mantis 2] from the Atmosphere library ended up in the cinema verite scene of an active cardiac arrest tended to by paramedics in _Необъяснимо, но факт: [1. В объятиях смерти]_. Andrey Zolotarev was asked to score this sequence, but before giving it to him, the editor probably just put the first placeholder track that fit there from the nearest library music disk he had. Eventually, Zolotarev finished the piece called [Клиническая смерть], a heartbreaking track with the emotional depth of a funeral, but Praying Mantis, a shallow sound of everyday tragedies, fit this noisy scene much better, so it was left where it was, with Zolotarev's track was reserved for more contemplative sequences. Decisions like these are probably made in editing rooms of big-budget movies all the time.
