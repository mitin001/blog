## Vastaamo CEO Ville Tapio says the database with patient mental health records got leaked because his IT left it accessible to the Internet

> It’s still not known how or why the database was left open to the internet and so poorly protected, but Tapio blames his IT team for altering the security settings at some point from around November 2017 until March 2019. He assumes it was to make it easier for them to get in and carry out tasks remotely from home.

[Ctrl + Alt + Chaos].

The weakest link in any organization's security is personnel. Instead of adding the networks of the remote workers to the allowlist (e.g., /32), they may leave it exposed to 0.0.0.0/0, i.e., all the networks on the Internet. That way, no matter how much the remote worker's IP address changes, they will still be able to access the database without having to modify its allowlist. But that's also how attackers breach firewalls. 0.0.0.0/0 covers every network: all the remote workers and also all the possible attackers. Performing an allowlist update for every network change is a small price to pay to keep malicious actors out of it.
