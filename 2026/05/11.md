## Non-lymphoid white blood cells

<img width="571" height="426" alt="17785149428717896345881772011711" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/386bc958-ddee-486a-bf5d-3e71ab2e3a17" />

[Plasma cell]

Not all white blood cells are of lymphoid origin. Granulocytes (basophils, neutrophils, and eosinophils) are non-lymphoid: they are more closely related to red blood cells and platelets. Meanwhile, plasma cells are of lymphoid origin, but their cancer is called multiple myeloma due to its bone-marrow location. Lymphoma is cancer of T cells or B cells that haven't differentiated into plasma cells.

## Giveaway scams

> Scammers impersonate a celebrity and promise to give away free bitcoin to whoever sends them some. They are easy to spot but when they are carried out on an account with tens of millions of followers like Musk’s, the fraudsters were bound to fool a small number of people.

[Ctrl + Alt + Chaos].

When someone manages to hack a celebrity's account, they are given immediate access to millions of people, the celebrity's fans. What does one do with all this attention? An unsophisticated scammer will do what they always do: play a numbers game. They send out an invitation to an obvious scam from the celebrity's account hoping that enough gullible people fall for it. And enough of them do.
