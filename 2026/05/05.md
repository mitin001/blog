## How the Vastaamo database got leaked by ransom_man

> The patient database was left directly exposed to the internet and unprotected for fifteen months. There was no firewall stopping people accessing it from any computer in the world. So, by using common internet scanning tools, anyone could have found it and attempted to break in. ... The private, personal and sensitive details of 33,000 therapy patients.

[Ctrl + Alt + Chaos].

Script kiddies from all over (like ransom_man) regularly check Shodan for web services with misconfigured access. The administrators for such services often either never bother to correctly restrict public access to them or simply lack the knowledge of how to do it or (the awareness of the danger they're putting their data in by not doing it). If anyone off the Internet can access your data, it's likely that someone will, and they will probably do so for a malicious purpose. Misconfiguring access to private data is like forgetting to lock the doors to your house, except worse. Not many criminals will be brazen enough to try a lock on a physical door to see if it's unlocked. And yet, the digital equivalent of this happens on the Internet every day.
