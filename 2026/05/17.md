## Freedom is not free, but let's make it cost money, not lives

> When I see people spouting slogans like "Peace, not war," "Unity, not division," I roll my eyes. Of course this is what we all want. The adult question is, at what price?

[Real Time with Bill Maher: 4/11/2026](https://podcastindex.org/podcast/742515?episode=53294584919), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=141e231a0c97f32ca36235dbda2e1c4c).

The mature 21st-century approach to world peace is not violence. It is global trade. Get people interested in putting aside their weapons so that they can make money. Peace should be the ultimate benefit of capitalism. Greed is less of a vice than violence.

## Blogs that make you cringe come from the place of insecurity

> I read the online archives of literary magazines that no longer existed, digitally window-shopped for clothing I could not afford, and created and abandoned private, aspirational blogs with names like A Meaningful Life, in the vain hope that they might push me closer to leading one.

[Uncanny Valley].

People who ache to dispense advice for how you should live your life are the last people we should be listening for such advice. Their burning desire to instruct others on the subject is from the knowledge, shoved deep in their hearts and minds by their inner selves, that their own lives need instruction.
