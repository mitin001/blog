## Cancer is a disease of aging

> The body normally eliminates cells with damaged DNA before they turn cancerous. But the body’s ability to do so goes down as we age. This is part of the reason why there is a higher risk of cancer later in life.

[What Is Cancer?]

An older person is more likely to get cancer, but that's not just because they've lived long enough to give the unlikely mutations more chances to occur. The immune system wears out as we age; the older one's immune system is, the more likely it is to let mutations go undetected and uncorrected.
