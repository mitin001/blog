## Hacking by trying the default password works more often than you'd think

> The Stuxnet attack on an Iranian nuclear facility made good use of the fact that the Siemens computers controlling the centrifuges used a default password.

[Modern Operating Systems], sec.9.4.1.

When you hear about a sophisticated and scary cyberattack on the news and decide to learn more about how it happened, you may just find that the victim never bothered to change the manufacturer's default password on its devices, so pretty much anyone should breach them. Sometimes, what makes an elite government hacking group is simply permission to do the hacking.
