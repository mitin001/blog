## Public-key cryptography

> Publish your public key and everyone can use it and be sure that you alone can read the message.

[Modern Operating Systems], sec.9.3.2.

In RSA and other public-key systems, encryption is public, fast, and simple, while decryption is private, slow, and computationally intensive. The difference in the computational intensity between encryption and decryption in public-key systems is like the difference between computing the square of a number (multiplying two numbers together) and computing the square root of a number. The reverse operations are not symmetrical in their computational complexity. The encryption in public-key systems uses a public key while the decryption uses a secret private key. Computing the private key from the public key and the encrypted message is thought to be computationally infeasible.

So maybe don't be your own financial advisor.
