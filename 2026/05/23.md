## Be your own financial advisor

> One of the greatest grips, I believe, legal grips in corporate history was the financial advisor. ... If you added up all alternative investments and hedge fund returns for the last 50 years, they have underperformed the S&P by the amount of their fees. ... Over the medium and long-term, they all underperform the market by their fees.

[Pivot: 5/8/2026](https://podcastindex.org/podcast/174725?episode=54380652635), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=bf5215e43b7d62366d04ea487025e445).

Listen to Scott Galloway. If you're fortunate enough to have disposable income, the smartest thing to do is to invest it back into the economy and watch your wealth compound. The second smartest thing to do is to pay a financial advisor to do this for you. The difference between these two approaches is your financial advisor's wage. In this way, part of your disposable income is paying for a service you could do yourself, but perhaps, it is better that way. It's one fewer unemployed person who also serves as a commercial for investing money into the market. Perhaps, this way the economy benefits from your wealth slightly more for the cost of you sharing some of its spoils. And why not share? It's disposable income anyway. It's meant to be shared. So maybe don't be your own financial advisor.
