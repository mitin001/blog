## Manufacturing demand by exploiting human vulnerabilities

> The most profitable businesses in the world tap into one thing, and that is a flaw in our instincts. We're desperate for affirmation, we're desperate for free gaming, we're desperate for opportunities to procreate. So we buy Ferraris, we go on Instagram, and we gamble. The highest margin products in the world tap into a flaw in our instincts.

[Pivot: 4/17/2026](https://podcastindex.org/podcast/174725?episode=53552119039), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=d7a20480ab5f6d4297711b647a5daada).

Like any complex system, humans can be hacked. When the goal is to separate humans from their money, large corporations induce us to assign worth to worthless things. That's how many of them have become large corporations in the first place. Meanwhile, we give in to manufactured demand. We buy things we don't need just because that's what everybody else does. We think these things make us attractive to others. We are made to think we can outsmart the house. But the house always wins. It doesn't matter if the product is useful as long as it's addictive.
