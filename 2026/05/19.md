## Metastasis of premalignant cells

<img width="730" height="468" alt="17792082627122766461219977116086" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/debcedf2-9c7e-457c-b887-768be17e3907" />

[The migration ability of stem cells].

Metastasis is not necessarily a late-stage occurrence with cancer. It has been proposed that premalignant stem cells could spread throughout the body before any tumor develops. In that case, there is no primary tumor, and the origin of the satellite, metastatic tumors are simply stem cell lines with deregulated cells.
