## The effect of the 20th-century racism on the American quality of life today

> A bunch of people in the US back then didn't want Black people, for example, to get these benefits. So no one got them. But in Europe, everyone gets the big three: vacation, pension, health care as a right.

[Vacation and why Americans take so little].

Racism is a big part of why the US still lags behind many developed countries on the quality of life. When citizens are busy trying to take rights away from what some view as the undeserving among them, they're distracted from negotiating for the rest of them getting those rights. Why not just team up with every group out there for the benefit of all?

## Mouthpieces for great thinkers

> ADMIRAL, n. That part of a war-ship which does the talking while the figure-head does the thinking.

[The Devil's Dictionary].

Division of labor doesn't always make sense in the modern workplace, or, as it turns out, in historical ones, too. Why do so many workplace hierarchies immediately go to separating one's mouth from their brain? Why do great thought leaders need to hire mouthpieces? What happened to the idea that one can only think clearly if they can express themselves clearly? If that's true, then why should we let someone else speak for a thinker? Why let those deep thoughts get scrambled in relay?
