## In a swatter's toolkit: FBI Buzzword List

> A detailed sheet, 800 lines long, of all the types of guns, ammunition and bombs that they would reference to convince responders that they were armed and dangerous. Categories like submachine guns and sniper rifles helped the hoaxers quote to police what weapons they had in a believable way.

[Ctrl + Alt + Chaos].

Swatting is a kind of social engineering where the target is not tech support but an emergency dispatcher. And the objective is not to take over the victim's account but to intimate the victim with SWAT teams dispatched to their house. Just as regular social engineering demands extensive preparation (open-source intelligence gathering), swatting requires the attacker to study up on weapons to sound convincing.

## _[Необъяснимо, но факт: 2. Кыштымский пришелец]_ ([Rutube]): a rerun of an old film about [Alyoshenka] sold to viewers like an original detective story

Some interviews are edited into this episode from a different film: Eвгений Mохичев, Тамара Просвирина. The background music in those interviews ([Vangelis - Thermo Vision]) is not the background music normally used for _Необъяснимо, но факт_. If one were to get a list of pre-2005 Russian films about the Kyshtym Dwarf and look at their spectrograms, one would be able to quickly find this film. _Thermo Vision_ has easily identifiable parallel lines on the spectrogram (e.g., at 500-600 Hz and 1100 Hz). Another track in the interview was [Peter Gabriel - Gethsemane] from the soundtrack for _The Last Temptation of Christ_. The soundtrack to this film has been used in *Внимание, розыск!* in the segment about [Радастея] that was later similarly edited into an episode of _Необъяснимо, но факт_. Perhaps, the interviews come from an unaired episode of *Внимание, розыск!* deemed by its network НТВ to stray far too into the tabloid territory to be worthy of their airtime. So, naturally, it found its home on ТНТ. In this episode, the sole track from the DeWolfe library, _The Third Eye_, was introduced into the _Необъяснимо, но факт_ score. It's aurally similar to the track from the interview with Сергей Просвирин. The editor was perhaps facing a challenge: how to sneek the host's commentary in between two interview segments without interrupting the background instrumental in a noticeable way. They probably went through several library disks looking for the right track before landing on DeWolfe's _The Third Eye_. This could explain them straying from their usual libraries: Megatrax, KOKA, Videohelper, Omni, CDM.
