## Paradox: the immorality of faith

> The more virtuous you think you are, the more you have permission to behave diabolically.

[Real Time with Bill Maher: 5/2/2026](https://podcastindex.org/podcast/742515?episode=54104396328), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=2dcb9633ade415f1706b79716aa6ad55).

Bret Stephens offers a paradoxical heuristic: be wary of those who extol themselves for their supposed virtue. A moment's reflection lets one see why there is nothing paradoxical about it. Bragging is a vice, and when one hurriedly submits to a vice to recall some latent virtue, it's no surprise that for such a person, there may be quite a few more black marbles on the scale. One needn't see far to meet such people. For example, faith often attracts people who rely on others to tell them what's moral, i.e., they have a weaker moral compass. And once the good book gives them the laws of morality, they are often quick to lecture others on this subject.
