## An authoritarian is a boundlessly selfish taker

> Like a proverbial goldfish, guys like these grow to fit the size of their bowl, which means the more they have to work with in terms of authoritarian power they can consolidate to themselves, the more resources they have at their disposal to use for nefarious authoritarian purposes, the more repressive and monstrous they can be.

[The Rachel Maddow Show: 5/12/2026](https://podcastindex.org/podcast/509873?episode=54808277441), [transcribed](https://writecomments.com/transcripts/?md5=86057fefcfed17a98391a8cf4914ac5f).

There's no limit to how much an authoritarian will take from his people. That's actually the part of his character that got him to the top. The world doesn't just hand anyone power and wealth. One has to take it from the world. The biggest takers are the heads of state. Theoretically, this doesn't apply to the states where the top job is defined by competing branches to be about serving the people, but even some states with checks and balances have been sliding toward authoritarianism lately. Democracy isn't automatic; shooing thieves away from the nation's purse is hard work, and it happens at the ballot box.
