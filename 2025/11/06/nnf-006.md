> Monument to Mikhail Lermontov.

-- https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g298484-d8319255-Reviews-Monument_to_Mikhail_Lermontov-Moscow_Central_Russia.html

<img src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/writecomments.com/nnf/006/0/00_01_32.680 frame=2317.jpg" height=150>

> Михаил Лермонтов любил на досуге заниматься математикой. Однажды, не справившись с трудной задачей, он лёг спать и во сне увидел незнакомого человека, который подсказал решение. Лермонтов проснулся, записал его, а заодно нарисовал портрет незнакомца. Через много лет, когда портрет попал в руки специалистов, они признали в нём Джона Непера, создателя логарифмов.

> Mikhail Lermontov enjoyed learning math in his spare time. Once, unable to solve a difficult problem, he went to bed and saw a stranger in his dream who gave him the answer. Lermontov woke up, wrote it down, and drew a portrait of the stranger. Many years later, when the portrait came into the hands of experts, they recognized it as John Napier, the discoverer of logarithms.

## What are the sources of background music for the film *Необъяснимо, но факт: 6. Вещий сон*?

Mostly library music from Videohelper (VH), Megatrax, CDM, Omnimusic (OMN), DeWolfe Music, and Universal Production Music: Atmosphere (ATMOS), KOKA Music (KOK), New York Beats (NYB). There is also original music by CJShurup and another composer known to Maxim Masaltsev. There is also music by Любаша, one of the subjects of the film. The track "Alexandrov Ensemble - Священная война" briefly plays during a segment on the history of USSR, and "Goratory - Pus Filled Maggots" plays as the host is depicted as having a nightmare.
